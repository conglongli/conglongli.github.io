Conglong Li received Ph.D. in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University in 2020. During his Ph.D. study, he was advised by Professor David G. Andersen. He received both B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science from Rice University in 2013 and 2014, respectively. During his study at Rice, he was advised by Professor Alan L. Cox and supported by the Graduate Research Fellowship.

In general, Conglong works on improving performance and resource efficiency of various computer systems.

In particular, he is currently focusing on the intersection of systems and machine learning:

  • Systems for ML: building efficient systems for ML applications.
  • ML for Systems: leveraging ML techniques to build intelligent systems.
